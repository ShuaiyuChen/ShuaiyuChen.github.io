Welcome! My name is Shuaiyu Chen.

I’m a Ph.D. in Finance at University of Rochester.

My research focuses on factor investing, asset management, short selling and security lending, and international equity markets. I am also interested in Fintech, especially machine learning and alternative data in quantitative investing.

Shuaiyu Chen

Ph.D. in Finance,

University of Rochester

I do theoretically-motivated empirical research, with an emphasis on issues relevant to practitioners. Specifically, I focus on the following topics:

  1. The behaviors and performance of portfolio managers
  2. The impact of belief dispersion and asymmetric information on short selling, lending fee, and stock return
  3. The role played by large institutional investors in corporate decisions
  4. The returns and risks to anomaly strategies in international markets

I am also interested in state-of-art machine learning methods and their ability to improve anomaly strategies' performance. In addition, I am working on a project to extract trading signals on firms' intangible assets by using the data of academic research affiliated with public firms.
